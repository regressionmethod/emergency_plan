🔊 Door Barking Emergency Reset
⚠️
DO NOT: Yell "quiet," repeatedly say "no," or open the door while they're barking. This reinforces the chaos loop.
  1. Interrupt with Body Language
    Turn sideways, avoid eye contact, take 3 deep breaths. Do NOT face your dog directly.
  2. Use Reset Cue
    Say "Oops" in a calm, low tone. Wait 3 seconds for ANY pause in barking.
  3. Redirect to Calm Zone
    The moment they pause, say "Let's go" and guide them to their crate or mat using gentle leash pressure (no pulling).
  4. Reward the Reset
    Once in their calm zone, give quiet praise and a small treat. Stay calm yourself — no excited energy.
  5. Wait Before Answering Door
    Don't answer the door until your dog is settled. This teaches them that calm behavior opens doors, not barking.
✅
Success Tip: Practice this sequence when there's NO doorbell. Use the cue "Oops" → "Let's go" during calm moments so your dog learns the pattern without stress.
🐕 Leash Lunging Emergency Reset
⚠️
DO NOT: Jerk the leash back hard, yell, or keep walking toward the trigger. This escalates the reaction.
  1. Stop and Turn
    The moment you see tension in the leash, stop walking. Turn your body 90 degrees away from the trigger.
  2. Create Distance
    Say "Let's go" and walk in the opposite direction. Use steady, gentle leash pressure — no yanking.
  3. Find Calm Space
    Move to a spot where your dog can see the trigger but isn't reactive (usually 10-20 feet back).
  4. Reset with Attention
    Say your dog's name once. When they look at you, immediately say "Yes" and give a small treat.
  5. Wait for Calm
    Don't move forward until your dog's body language is relaxed. This might take 30 seconds to 2 minutes.
✅
Success Tip: Practice the "name → look → treat" sequence at home first. Your dog needs to know this cue works BEFORE you use it during stress.
💧 Indoor Accident Emergency Reset
⚠️
DO NOT: Scold, rub their nose in it, or show frustration. Accidents during regression are stress responses, not defiance.
  1. Stay Calm
    Take a deep breath. Your energy sets the tone. If you're stressed, your dog will absorb that.
  2. Clean Without Drama
    Use enzyme cleaner to remove all scent traces. Do this calmly — don't make it a big event.
  3. Reset the Schedule
    Take your dog out every 2 hours for the next 3 days, even if they don't seem to need it.
  4. Supervised Freedom Only
    Keep your dog in the same room as you. If you can't supervise, use crate or tether them near you.
  5. Reward Outdoor Success
    The moment they go outside, give immediate praise and a treat. Make outdoor elimination the best thing ever.
✅
Success Tip: Accidents often spike during schedule changes or stress. Return to a puppy-like schedule temporarily — it's not permanent.
⬆️ Jumping & Overstimulation Emergency Reset
⚠️
DO NOT: Push them down, knee them, or give attention (even negative attention). This often makes jumping worse.
  1. Remove Your Attention
    Turn your back, cross your arms, and look up at the ceiling. Become "boring" immediately.
  2. Wait for Four Paws
    Don't turn around until all four paws are on the ground for at least 3 seconds.
  3. Reward Calm
    The moment they're calm, turn around and say "Yes" in a quiet voice. Give attention or treats only when they're settled.
  4. Practice Calm Greetings
    Have family members practice coming through the door calmly. Reset every time jumping starts.
  5. Create a Landing Spot
    Teach your dog to go to a specific mat or area when people enter. Practice this during calm moments first.
✅
Success Tip: Jumping is usually attention-seeking or overstimulation. Lower your energy when you come home — calm hellos create calm dogs.
🏃 Recall Failure Emergency Reset
⚠️
DO NOT: Repeat "come" multiple times, chase your dog, or use an angry voice. This teaches them that "come" is optional.
  1. Stop Repeating the Cue
    Say "come" once in a happy voice. If they don't respond within 5 seconds, don't repeat it.
  2. Make Yourself Interesting
    Run in the opposite direction, clap your hands, or make exciting noises. Don't chase — make them chase YOU.
  3. Use Environmental Help
    If possible, move toward a door, car, or treat bag. Use things your dog already wants to come to.
  4. Reward Any Movement
    The moment they take a step toward you, say "Yes!" enthusiastically. Reward partial success.
  5. Practice in Boring Places
    For the next week, only practice recall in low-distraction areas where success is guaranteed.
✅
Success Tip: Recall fails when it competes with something more interesting. Make coming to you the BEST thing that happens to your dog.
💥 Destructive Behavior Emergency Reset
⚠️
DO NOT: Punish after the fact, leave tempting items out, or assume it's spite. Destruction is usually anxiety or boredom.
  1. Interrupt Calmly
    If you catch them in the act, say "Oops" and redirect to an appropriate chew toy immediately.
  2. Remove Temptation
    Put away shoes, remotes, cushions — anything they might destroy. Management prevents bad habits from forming.
  3. Provide Legal Outlets
    Give them puzzle toys, frozen Kongs, or appropriate chews. Tired mouths don't destroy furniture.
  4. Increase Supervision
    Keep them in the same room as you. Use baby gates or tethers if needed. Prevent practice of bad behavior.
  5. Address the Root Cause
    Increase physical exercise, mental stimulation, or consider if separation anxiety is the trigger.
✅
Success Tip: Most destruction happens in the first 20 minutes alone or when dogs are understimulated. Tire them out before leaving.
